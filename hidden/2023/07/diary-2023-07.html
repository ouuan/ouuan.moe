Note: Why are my default branches called “master”

In this article, I’ll try to explain the reasons why I don’t change the name of my default Git branches to “main”.

“master” as a branch name is not evil：

  • “master” as an adjective has nothing to do with slavery

    The word “master” has many different meanings, among which the only one related to slavery is a noun. In the phrase “master branch”, the adjective “master” cannot possibly be understood as something related to slavery.

  • The master branch is a branch, not a person, nor a master

    It’s just a branch. It definitely cannot be the master of a person. In practice, the relationship between the master branch and other branches is not as it is between a master and their slaves. So the master branch is not a master.

  • It is not words but how people are treated that matters

    I have no problem if you call employers “masters” and call employees “slaves” as long as the “slaves” are paid, respected, and there is no forced labor. But I don’t want to be an “employee” who is actually doing forced labor.

  • What we don’t like is not what to be ignored

    Some people may argue, “I know ‘master branch’ has nothing to do with slavery, but I just hate this word.” But I don’t think we should simply ignore what we don’t like. We should face the problem. Ignoring the word “master” doesn’t help people fight against slavery.
